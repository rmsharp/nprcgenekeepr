## SESSION PROTOCOL — FOLLOW BEFORE DOING ANYTHING

Read and follow `SESSION_RUNNER.md` step by step. It is your operating
procedure for every session. It tells you what to read, when to stop,
and how to close out.

**Three rules you will be tempted to violate:** 1. **Orient first** —
Read SAFEGUARDS.md → SESSION_NOTES.md → run `methodology_dashboard.py` →
git status → report findings → WAIT FOR THE USER TO SPEAK 2. **1 and
done** — One deliverable per session. When it’s complete, close out. Do
not start the next thing. 3. **Auto-close** — When done: evaluate
previous handoff, self-assess, document learnings, write handoff notes,
commit, report, STOP.

`SESSION_RUNNER.md` documents known failure modes and their
countermeasures. The protocol compensates for documented tendencies to
skip orientation, skip close-out, and continue past the deliverable.
